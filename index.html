About

Scholar of Water,
Cities & Power

Ayushi Chauhan is a PhD Candidate in the Department of History at Boston University, where she specializes in South Asian and environmental history. Her research sits at the intersection of histories of water, disease, development, space, and governance.

Her dissertation examines how water made and remade India's capital city over a century and a half of political change, newly accounting for the many tensions between environmental governance and the imagination of shared spaces. In doing so, it argues that interventions around water are central to a deeply contextualized history of governance, contingency, vision, inequality, and development in the colonial and postcolonial world.

She received a Master's in Development Studies from the Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati, and a B.A. in History and Political Science from Miranda House, Delhi University. She has independently conducted primary research in archives across India, the United Kingdom, and the United States, supported by numerous fellowships and grants.

Research

Areas of Inquiry

02

Environmental Governance

Exploring tensions between environmental governance and the imagination of shared urban spaces, tracing how state interventions shaped inequality and development across South Asian cities.

03

Disease, Sanitation & Colonial Urbanism

Examining epidemic disease, colonial administration, and urban transformation — from cholera in the nineteenth century to sanitary reform in the planning of New Delhi in the twentieth.

04

Rivers & Urban Commons

Investigating the commodification of urban commons in India, with attention to how rivers like the Yamuna were altered by development pressures and contested by communities across Delhi, Lucknow, and Gujarat.

05

South Asian Modernity

Situating India's urban transformations within broader histories of colonial modernity, infrastructural development, and transnational knowledge about water, sanitation, and public health.

06

Archival Research

Expertise in archives across the National Archives of India, British Library (London), University of Edinburgh, National Archives (Washington DC), and Rockefeller Library (New York).
